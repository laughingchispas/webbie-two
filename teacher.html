teaching background

I have been teaching for nearly 10 years in primary and private schools in the US, France and Spain as well at university and in adult education in Belgium. While I was trained as a Suzuki violin teacher in the US, I have also been trained in a number of Education Ministries in Europe, opening my mind to a variety of teaching pedagogies.

musical training

My musical training began at the age of 6 at the Eastman School of Music. From there, a Suzuki Violin student myself, I continued to study violin through University. Currently, I am completing viola studies at the Royal Conservatory of Antwerp.

music teacher

As a music teacher, my job is to discover how each student learns and cater my teaching style to each of them. I am encouraging, supportive, and positive; I understand that each child will learn at his or her own pace.

In class, I demonstrate and model proper techniques and posture and give suggestions on how to practice at home.

fun & discipline

Mostly importantly, I will strive to always keep the lessons active and fun; creating music should always be enjoyable for each of us.

student, teacher, parent triangle

An important distinction between traditional pedagogy and the Suzuki Method is the involvement of parents in the learning process. The Teacher/Parent/Student relationship as described by the Suzuki Philosophy is an equilateral triangle. Each part is equally important in sustaining a healthy relationship and cultivating optimal growth of the studentâ€™s musical education. The three units must work together toward the same goal, relating with mutual respect.

Knowing the importance of a parent's role in a child's musical development, I will teach the parent so that they are confident in how to help the student during practice during the week.

individual lessons

I encourage children to have one individual class a week, even in the summer. This creates a consistent learning environment that favors progress

group lessons

Another important pillar to Suzuki education in the group class. The aspect of learning in a group, like other activities for children (soccer, dance, etc.), creates a peer-peer environment, foster relationships between children, and foments a natural form of motivation and enjoyment to playing like no other.

the communicative method

All my language classes are based on the communicative method of language acquisition whereby students are immersed in situations that mirror real-life situations and therefore provide the students with the tools to use the English they have learned in class out in the world.
