background & training

I have been teaching for nearly 10 years in primary and private schools in the US, France and Spain as well at university and in adult education in Belgium. While I was trained as a Suzuki violin teacher in the US, I have also been trained in a number of Education Ministries in Europe, opening my mind to a variety of teaching pedagogies.

I am encouraging, supportive, and positive; I understand that each child will learn at his or her own pace. In class, I demonstrate and model proper techniques and posture and give suggestions on how to practice at home.

Mostly importantly, I will strive to always keep the lessons active and fun; creating music should always be enjoyable for each of us.

individual lessons

I encourage children to have one individual class a week, even in the summer; a consistent learning environment that favors progress.

group lessons

Another important pillar to Suzuki education in the group class. The aspect of learning in a group, like other activities for children (soccer, dance, etc.), creates a peer-peer environment, foster relationships between children, and foments a natural form of motivation and enjoyment to playing like no other.

parents, active not passive

An important distinction between traditional pedagogy and the Suzuki Method is the involvement of parents in the learning process.

personal note

In today's world, I feel we have less and less 'real' 'human' contact with our family members and friends. Nowhere is this more a problem than with the relationship we have with our children. Having time every single day with our children that doesn't revolve around the dialy routine, eating, getting ready for school, homework, meals etc. is crucial. Creating an atmosphere where we teach discipline and commitment to an activity that we may not always feel like doing, but for which we will see progress, is sadly missing in many young people's lives. This is a gift you give your child, your family, and yourself.
