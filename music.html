training and interests

My musical journey began at the Suzuki Early Education department at the Eastman School of Music in New York state. From there, a young childhood interest turned into my passion, my raison d'être, a lifestyle. Music is simply something I cannot, and will not live without. At university, I studied violin performance with Walter Schwede as well as Mathematics and Languages at Western Washington University. Following my bachelors, I was fortunate enough to travel the world and fell in love with many forms of world music, other cultures and languages. This influence can be seen in my love of folk music and working with international projects. Later, I decided to make the switch to viola (where I honestly feel much more at home) studying viola with Tony Nys at the Royal Conservatory of Brussels and Leo de Neve at the Royal Conservatory of Antwerp. My musical interests include chamber music, minimalist forms, pop music, folk, tango, jazz, voice, *teaching suzuki, and string arranging.

*For more on Suzuki Education, go to my teaching page.
